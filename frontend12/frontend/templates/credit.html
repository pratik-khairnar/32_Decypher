Key Observations

  • Skewness: The distribution is slightly right-skewed, indicating a longer tail to the right.
  • Modality: The distribution appears to be unimodal, with a single peak around the credit score range of 700-750.
  • Range: The credit scores range from approximately 500 to 900.
  • Density: The density of the distribution is highest around the peak, indicating that more individuals have credit scores within that range.

Implications

  • Majority in Good Credit Range: The peak around 700-750 suggests that a significant portion of the population has good credit scores.
  • Fewer with Excellent Scores: The right-skewness indicates that fewer individuals have exceptional credit scores.
  • Risk Assessment: Lenders can use this information to assess the creditworthiness of potential borrowers.
