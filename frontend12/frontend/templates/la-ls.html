Key Observations

  • Median Loan Amounts: Approved loans generally have a lower median loan amount compared to defaulted loans.
  • Distribution: The distribution of loan amounts for defaulted loans is more spread out, with a higher interquartile range (IQR), indicating greater variability.
  • Outliers: There are a few outliers (data points significantly different from the majority) in both groups, but they are more prominent in the defaulted loans.
  • Overlap: The distributions overlap to some extent, suggesting that loan amount alone might not be a strong predictor of default.

Implications for Loan Eligibility Prediction

  • Consider Other Factors: While loan amount provides some insight, other factors like credit score, income, and debt-to-income ratio should be considered for a more accurate prediction.
  • Risk Assessment: The higher variability in loan amounts for defaulted loans suggests a potential need for more stringent risk assessment measures for larger loans.
  • Feature Engineering: Exploring interactions between loan amount and other variables might reveal additional patterns that can improve prediction accuracy.
